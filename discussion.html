Post-Disturbance Tree Community Trajectories in a Neotropical Forest

4 Discussion

The long-term survey of tropical forest plots after disturbance demonstrated a significant impact on stands composition and abundance distribution lasting for more than 30 years. Disturbance induced environmental filters favoring acquisitive functional types and enhancing communities evenness. Plots response was highly variable and might depend on its initial conditions.

  • The disturbance significantly impact the functional and taxonomic composition and diversity of the stands for more than 30 years. > Question Hubbel vs. Molino: IDH or not, if the composition is affected the changes are oriented then the IDH is valid. > Recovery of the composition, probably not much external arrivals (seed mass characteristics as in storm regime (???) the recovery is probably due to the coexistence of species. Sometimes recovery is only due to resprouting and regrowth of species in place (Zimmerman et al. 1994) > Higher seed mass = more sensitive to fragmentation because of the dispersers ecology (Cramer, Mesquita, and Bruce Williamson 2007) > Hubbell: identical species abundance repartition so the recruitment is random. This is the natural regime: we also proved that.The disturbance changes the natural disturbance regime?

  • Important differences among plots of same treatments: possible impact of initial diversity composition, local abiotic parameters (topography, soils, surrounding stands). Impacts depend on the disturbance intensity, but further parameters may be involved. > Composition can change the environmental recovery: resprouting and seedling dynamics are highly dependant of taxonomic composition (Paciorek et al. 2000) > The recovery dynamics largely depend on the past disturbance regime also (???)

  • The shift in functional composition differs from the null model: biotic and abiotic filters are at stake but only tangible from 10/15 years after logging. > Discussion with Cannon, 1998: they found no different process than rarefaction for 8 years after logging, but the time might have been too short -> divergence due to recruitment and not rarefaction after random species disappearance. Same conclusions otherwise. > hubbel stop the observation 13 years after disturbance, which is very short (Hubbell et al. 1999; Sheil and Burslem 2003), and according to our observatoin this is not the peak period yet

  • Disturbance involves new filters for species settling in the stands, probably according to their functional strategy. Disturbance generates a functional shift of communities toward acquisitive strategies. Ecosystems function rely on abundant species: changes in stands functioning is then expected.
    > (Flores, 2006 for differential sapling success after disturbance) > typical pattern of the IDH (Sheil and Burslem 2003) with an initial rise of the diversity followed by a decline through compoetitive domination. IDH is a regression to previous successional states and the more intense the logging the further we go back.The overall diversit increases because the IDH is patchy > Acquisitive like functional traits associated with higher diversity and disturbance dynamics (TerSteege and Hammond 2001). Increase in functional diversity correlated with shifts toward more disturbance-like functional traits.

  • If the recovery is not achieved 30 years after disturbance, it is on the way to. Only one plot displayed a still increasing Simpson diversity, and all plots showed a stabilization or a decrease of the composition distancing from 1989 inventories. however this is the impact of a single disturbance event: the repetition may have different impact (Olander, Bustamante, and Asner (2005) demonstrated the impact of logging on soils nutrient and compaction) > the IDH involves a unimodal response to disturbance (Sheil and Burslem 2003) and the functional strategy follows this pattern, however the composition still didn’t recover,neither did the taxo diversity. Functioning recovering faster than taxo?

  • To go further we should focus on recruitment which would also highlight the mechanisms underlying these shifts and dynamics and explicit the environmental filter. > Discussion about the composition impact: do we regenerate SB and species from old-growth forest (see (Sheil and Burslem 2003) for discussion).
