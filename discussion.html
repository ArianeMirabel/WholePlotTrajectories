Post-Disturbance Diversity Trajectories in a Neotropical Forest

4 Discussion

  • The disturbance significantly impact the functional and taxonomic composition and diversity of the stands for more than 30 years
  • Important differences among plots of same treatments: possible impact of initial diversity composition, local abiotic parameters (topography, soils, surrounding stands). Impacts depend on the disturbance intensity, but further parameters may be involved.
  • The shift in functional composition differs from the null model: biotic and abiotic filters are at stake but only tangible from 10/15 years after logging. Discussion with Cannon, 1998: they found no different process than rarefaction for 8 years after logging, but the time might have been too short-> divergence due to recruitment and not rarefaction after random species disappearance. Same conclusions otherwise.
  • Disturbance involves new filters for species settling in the stands, probably according to their functional strategy. Disturbance generates a functional shift of communities toward acquisitive strategies. Ecosystems function rely on abundant species: changes in stands functioning is then expected. (Flores, 2006 for differential sapling success after disturbance).
  • If the recovery is not achieved 30 years after disturbance, it is on the way to. Only one plot displayed a still increasing Simpson diversity, and all plots showed a stabilization or a decrease of the composition distancing from 1989 inventories.
  • To go further we should focus on recruitment which would also highlight the mechanisms underlying these shifts and dynamics and explicit the environmental filter.
