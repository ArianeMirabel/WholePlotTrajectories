Post-Disturbance Tree Community Trajectories in a Neotropical Forest

3 Results

Over time, 104423 trees and 696 botanical species were recorded. The disturbance proved to significantly impact for more than 30 years the functional and taxonomic composition and diversity of the stands.

3.0.1 Taxonomic and functional composition

Composition trajectories _ Trajectories of taxonomic and functional composition after disturbance were examined through the ordination of successive inventories from 1989 (5 years after disturbance) in a two-dimensional space based on NMDS analysis. Classifications were based on either abundance-based Bray-Curtis or incidence-based Jaccard dissimilarity (data not shown).

Both taxonomic and functional composition were substantially affected by disturbance and positively correlated with its intensity. The distance from the initial condition was significantly time-dependent (P<0.01). The taxonomic composition remained significantly different from the 1989 reference inventory until 30 years for all disturbed plots . From 20 years after disturbance, though, the distancing decreased which suggested a return toward the reference 1989 inventory and its resilience to disturbance ,. The functional composition distancing from the reference inventory was also positively correlated with disturbance intensity and similarly stabilized or reduced (for 5 of T1, T2 and T3 plots) from 20 years after disturbance . The coordinates of the functional traits were measured in the two-dimensional ordination space that mapped plots evolution along time (graphs not showed) which revealed that the trajectories of disturbed plots along time headed toward acquisitive functional strategies (from high SWG to high SLA and chlorophyll content).

3.0.2 Disturbance impact on diversity

Taxonomic diversity _ Trajectories of Richness, Shannon and Simpson taxonomic diversity were examined in relation to the 1994 inventories . For the three indices the OLS regression confirmed the time-dependency of the diversity variations (P<0.01) and a covariance analysis confirmed the significant effect of the disturbance treatment (P<0.01).

Figure 3.4: Trajectories of the difference to reference inventory, 10 years after disturbance, until 30 years after disturbance for plots (a) Richness, (b) Shannon and (c) Simpson diversity. Line colors represent the disturbance treatment (green for control, blue for T1,orange for T2 and red for T3). The average value of the 4 leaf traits (Leaf thickness, chlorophyll content, toughness and specific area) and the 2 stem traits (Wood density and barck thickness) are drawn along with the 0.025 and 0.975 percentile observed for 50 iteration of the taxonomic uncertainty propagation and the missing trait value filling processes.

The evolution along time of the difference of Richness, Shannon and Simpson diversity compared to reference inventories in 1989 remained even for 30 years after disturbance for the control plots whereas disturbed plots showed significant increase, positively correlated with the disturbance intensity . The richness stabilized for all disturbed plots from 20 years after disturbance . The Shannon and Simpson diversity also stabilized 20 years after disturbance for T1 and T2 plots whereas for 2 out of 3 plots T3 the Shannon and Simpson diversity continued increasing until 30 years after disturbance, illustrating a significant increase in communitiesâ€™ evenness.

Functional diversity _ The evolution along time of the Rao functinal diversity compared to reference inventories in 1989 remained even all along the survey for the control plots. All disturbed plots however showed an increase of the functional diversity that was positively correlated to the disturbance intensity . 30 after disturbance the diversity of disturbed plots remained significantly higher than this of control plots, thus whenever the initial disturbance intensity. The slope of the initial diversity increase and the maximum diversity were both positively correlated with the disturbance intensity. The OLS regression confirmed the time-dependency of the diversity variations (P<0.01) and the covariance analysis confirmed the significant effect of the disturbance treatment (P<0.01). The trajectories of plot 7 from the T1 disturbance treatment was discarded because of too high taxonomic uncertainty preventing correct analysis.
