Post-Disturbance Tree Community Trajectories in a Neotropical Forest

4 Discussion

Our study underlined the significant but contrasting impact of disturbance on tropical forests taxonomic and functional diversity. Specifically we unveiled an important decoupling between taxonomic evenness and functional characteristic of the communities, and by extension ecosystem functioning. This highlighted the functional overlap between species in tropical forests,typical of their huge biodiversity, and intricately questioned the different facets of communities resilience.

In line with the assumtions of the IDH our 30 years survey confirmed the significant increase of communities evenness and functional richness after disturbance. As it was demonstrated for the conditions 10 years after disturbance (???) the directional changes in communities composition reflected environmental filtering after disturbance which would support the IDH as demonstrated in (???) rather than pure stochastic dynamics as suggested in (???). The trajectories shaping a unimodal response to disturbance suggested the recovery of communities initial state as it was already suggested by the previous studies from the same site 10 years (???) and 20 years (Baraloto et al. 2012) after disturbance. Only one plot displayed a still increasing Simpson diversity, and all plots showed a stabilization or a decrease of the composition distancing from 1989 inventories. This recovery corroborate previous post-disturbance dynamics observed in a Bornean rainforest (???) where at large scale the richness was preserved although it was diminished locally or in Solomon islands where stands demonstrated a fast recovery and identical composition (???).

Although recovery seemed on its way the trajectories revealed significant changes in the ecological rules shaping communities over the 30 years of survey. Disturbance is thought to shift exclusive competition favoring competitive species to environmental filtering selecting acquisitive and disturbance resistant species (TerSteege and Hammond 2001; ???; Haddad et al. 2008) The comparison of observed trajectories to a null model with random traits set shuffling showed an oriented functional shift after disturbance which suggest additional biotic and abiotic filters for the 10 to 15 years after disturbance. In particular this is demonstrated by the evolution of seed mass classes proportions revealing a clear increase in small seeded and then large dispersive species. Faster than for taxonomic composition and diversity though, functional characteristic returned to their initial values and the values observed for control plots. This suggested some functional overlap in the pre-disturbance condition (i.e the usual tropical forests conditions). Such overlap and maybe functional versatility was already observed in highly diverse ecosystems (Bellwood et al. 2006) and sugegsted as one mechanisms for high diversity maintenance. Decoupled taxonomic and functional trajectories questions to what extent communities recovery is completed: ecosystem functioning might be quickly recoverd, and taxonomic composition following suit although along for much longer, but the resilience of the community itself might be altered as functional overlap themselves would be lost. Along with probably other environmental modifications (like soils nutrient cycling and compaction (Olander, Bustamante, and Asner 2005)) with alteration of communities resilience makes us expect different trajectories after subsequent disturbance. As disturbance seemed to update communities toward more disturbance resistant ones (Haddad et al. 2008) it is possible that eventually the disturbance regie observed has set another permanent condition, which would only be more diverse if good competitors, poor disturbance resitant survive. > (Flores, 2006 for differential sapling success after disturbance)

  • To go beyond whole community diversity and highlight the ecological mechanisms behind communities assembly and repond to the question of their resilience we should focus on recruitment. It would also refine the role of environmental filters shaping communities assembly.
